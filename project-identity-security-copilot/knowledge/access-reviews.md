# Access Review Decision Guidance

## Baseline expectation

Access reviews should be evidence-driven. Reviewers should evaluate role sensitivity, business justification, recent activity, ownership, and exception history.

## Approve signals

- current business need is documented
- recent activity supports continued access
- the principal has a clear owner or manager
- the role assignment scope is narrow and justified

## Remove or escalate signals

- no recent evidence of need
- privileged access without compensating controls
- guest access that is stale or has weak sponsorship
- inconsistent justification across approvals and review notes

## Copilot answer boundary

The assistant may recommend approve, remove, or escalate, but the final decision belongs to the reviewer. The answer should cite the grounded evidence sections used to form the recommendation.
